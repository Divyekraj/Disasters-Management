2016 Indian heat wave

In April and May 2016, India experienced a major heat wave. A national record high temperature of 51.0 °C (123.8 °F) was set in the town of Phalodi, in the state of Rajasthan.[1][2] Over 1,600 people died with 30 million affected to some degree. There were also water shortages with drought worsening the impact of the heat wave.
